# Mis-Selling Incident Levels — Sales Call Flagging Framework

> **Purpose:** This document defines the three standardized incident severity levels (L1, L2, L3) to be used when auditing and flagging sales call recordings for mis-selling violations. It is the single source of truth for QA auditors, Team Leads, Sales Managers, and automated call-tagging workflows.
>
> **Scope:** All inbound and outbound sales calls across all programs (IIM, IIT, XLRI, and other partner institute programs offered through Accredian).
>
> **Note on Fee Statements:** All program fees are subject to 18% GST. Sales representatives may state the fee as "Base Amount + GST" OR as a "Total Inclusive Amount" (Base * 1.18). Both are accurate and must not be flagged. For example, stating an application fee as "₹11,800" is correct if the base fee is "₹10,000 + GST".

## Quick Reference Table

| Level | Name | Nature | Immediate Action |
|-------|------|---------|-----------------|
| **L1** | Minor Violation | Warning & Training | Formal warning email + coaching |
| **L2** | Major Violation | Suspension & TL Accountability | Suspension (7–14 days) + formal review |
| **L3** | Critical Violation | Termination & TL Escalation | Immediate termination + possible legal action |

## L1 — Minor Violation *(Warning & Training)*

### Definition
An L1 incident involves minor miscommunication, a lapse in script adherence, or isolated vague/misleading statements that cause customer confusion but do not constitute deliberate deception. These are errors of omission or imprecision rather than intentional misrepresentation.

### Criteria
- Minor miscommunication about program features
- Lack of clarity that leads to customer confusion
- Isolated instances of vague or non-specific information
- Failure to follow standard sales scripts or introduction guidelines

### Specific Violation Examples

#### 1. Script Adherence Failures — Introduction
- Failing to mention the **full and correct company name "Accredian"** during the call introduction
  - *Example:* Rep says "This is Payal, calling regarding the XLRI Strategic HR Leadership Program" — without naming Accredian
- Failing to mention the **complete and accurate institute name**
  - *Example:* Saying "IIT Kanpur" instead of "E&ICT Academy IIT Kanpur"
  - *Example:* Saying "IIT-G" instead of "E&ICT Academy, IIT Guwahati"
  - *Example:* Saying "IIM Visakhapatnam" when full name is required in context
- Referring to a program by an abbreviated or informal name instead of the full registered program name

#### 2. Vague or Non-Specific Commitments
- Stating approximate module/campus counts without precision
  - *Example:* Saying "2 or 3 days of campus immersion" when the correct commitment is a fixed "2-day campus immersion"
- Making generic outcome claims without factual grounding
  - *Example:* Promising "multiple capstone projects" when only one is offered
- Not specifying attendance criteria when the customer directly asks about missing classes

#### 3. Topic-Level Miscommitments (Isolated)
- Mentioning incorrect number of terms, modules, or program components in isolation
  - *Example:* Stating there are "2 BYOP projects" when the program has only 1
  - *Example:* Stating sessions happen "only in the first 6 months" when sessions run throughout
- Providing incorrect batch size or start dates
  - *Example:* Mentioning batch size as "60–70" when the correct figure differs
- Sharing incorrect attendance percentage requirements
  - *Example:* Stating "70% attendance is mandatory" without verification of the actual threshold

#### 4. Incomplete Program Discussion
- Failing to cover program offerings, curriculum, or key differentiators during a counselling call
- Not discussing program-specific inclusions and exclusions (e.g., campus immersion fees)

### Actions — Sales Representative
- **Formal Warning Email** issued to the Sales Rep and their Team Lead
- **Mandatory training/coaching session** on correct communication, script adherence, and program knowledge
- **Close monitoring** of all future sales calls for a defined period

### Actions — Team Lead (TL)
- **Advisory Warning** issued to the TL for lack of supervision
- TL is **required to conduct a coaching session** for the team covering the identified gaps
- **Increased audit frequency** for the team for 30 days post-incident

### Instance Counter Trigger
- Each individual call where one or more L1 violations are found counts as **1 instance** on the Rep's Instance Counter.
- Accumulation of **3 or more L1 instances** by the same rep escalates review consideration to L2.

## L2 — Major Violation *(Suspension & TL Accountability)*

### Definition
An L2 incident involves deliberate or reckless misrepresentation of key program features, false commitments that materially affect the customer's purchase decision, unethical pressure tactics, or deliberate omission of important terms and conditions. These violations damage customer trust and pose a risk to brand reputation and legal compliance.

### Criteria
- Misrepresentation of key program features (e.g., false job placement claims)
- False claims about faculty, their credentials, or affiliations
- Unethical pressure-selling tactics used to force or coerce a decision
- Deliberate omission of important terms and conditions (refund policies, eligibility criteria, fee structures)
- Any action that damages brand reputation

### Specific Violation Examples

#### 1. False Commitments on Fees & Refunds
- Stating that **application or program fees are refundable** when no such policy exists
  - *Example:* "If the documents get rejected, this ₹11,800 will be refunded back to you" — this is a false commitment
- Claiming that **campus immersion is included in the program fee** when it is charged separately
  - *Example:* Telling a learner that program fees include campus immersion for E&ICT IIT Kanpur programs
- Misrepresenting **EMI options or installment structures** to make the program seem more affordable

#### 2. False Claims About Faculty & Curriculum Delivery
- Claiming that **IIT/IIM/XLRI faculty directly teach the sessions** when sessions are delivered by industry experts (check this with the course details and flag ONLY if false. Some institute courses have their own faculty)
  - *Example:* "Classes will be taken by our faculty" — implying Accredian or partner institute faculty, when it is industry experts
- Mentioning **faculty names or credentials not listed** in the official factsheet or brochure
- Stating curriculum is **designed and delivered by the IIT itself** when it is not
  - *Example:* Confirming "the delivery and designing of the curriculum is by IIT itself" in response to a learner's question

#### 3. False or Misleading Placement & Career Assistance Claims
- Claiming **placement guarantees** or **assured salary hikes** (e.g., 40%, 50%, 60% hike) upon program completion
- Stating **100% outcome/placement rates** without factual basis
  - *Example:* "There are 100% outcomes for this program" and "it has given 80–180% hike on salary"
- Promising specific career services that are not formally offered
  - *Example:* "Accredian will line up interviews with top companies"
  - *Example:* "A separate B2B team assists with resume grooming, LinkedIn updates, and interview scheduling"
- Committing to an **entire month of career assistance** when no such fixed duration is guaranteed
- Promising **one-on-one interview coaches, career coaches, resume building, and job references** as guaranteed deliverables beyond what is contractually committed

#### 4. Misrepresentation of Brand / Institute Identity
- Using shortened or incorrect institute names that misrepresent the partner affiliation
  - *Example:* Saying "IIT Guwahati" instead of "E&ICT IIT Guwahati"
  - *Example:* Claiming "IIMV Alumni status" when the correct term is "Executive Alumni status of IIM Visakhapatnam" (Always check the course curriculum and ONLY flag if false)
- Claiming a **Graduation Certificate from IIT Guwahati** when the program awards a different credential
- Misrepresenting the correct Alumni status title

#### 5. Eligibility & Admissions Misrepresentation
- Confirming a prospect is eligible when they **clearly do not meet stated eligibility criteria**
  - *Example:* Confirming eligibility to an 11th standard student when the program requires graduation or 1+ year work experience
- Advising an ineligible applicant to **misrepresent their experience** on the application form
  - *Example:* "You can mention 1 year of experience" to a student who has none
- Claiming to **bypass or waive admission tests** (e.g., IITG screening tests)

#### 6. Access & Content Misrepresentation
- Promising **lifetime LMS access** when access is time-limited (e.g., 3 years)
  - *Example:* Stating "4-year access to LMS portal" when it is only 3 years
- Misaligning the customer with the wrong program cohort or wrong learning objectives
  - *Example:* Discussing one program during counselling but enrolling the learner in a different one

#### 7. Incorrect Cohort or Batch Information
- Providing the **wrong batch number**, batch size, or batch start date
- Setting incorrect cohort expectations that don't match the program the learner will be enrolled into

#### 8. Exceptional Written Commitments Without Approval
- Providing any written or verbal commitment that is **not aligned with the official factsheet, brochure, or protocol** and has not been pre-approved by management

### Actions — Sales Representative
- **Immediate Suspension of 7–14 days**
- **Formal Review Meeting** with the TL and Sales Manager
- **Final Warning Letter** issued for repeat offenses
- **Mandatory ethical sales training** before return to work

### Actions — Team Lead (TL)
- **Formal Written Warning** for lack of supervisory oversight
- Required to conduct a **full compliance review** of the entire team
- **Incentive/bonus may be impacted** for 15 or more days following the incident

### Instance Counter Trigger
- Each L2 incident adds **1 instance** to both the Rep's and TL's Instance Counters.
- Two or more L2 incidents by the same rep triggers escalation to L3 review.

## L3 — Critical Violation *(Termination & TL Escalation)*

### Definition
An L3 incident represents the most severe category of mis-selling. It involves deliberate identity fraud, impersonation, repeated violations despite prior warnings, or a single call where more than 4 L2 violations are found together — collectively constituting gross misconduct that causes severe and potentially irreparable damage to customer trust, brand reputation, or legal standing.

### Criteria
- **Falsifying identity / Impersonation** of an institution, its faculty, or program managers
- **More than 4 L2 violations found within the same call or case**
- **Repeated violations** — 4 or more prior warnings on record (across any severity level)
- Severe brand damage that leads to or risks **legal action or major reputational harm**
- **Customer escalations to the partner institute directly** involving rude, abusive, or threatening behaviour

### Specific Violation Examples

#### 1. Identity Fraud & Impersonation
- Representing oneself as being **from IIM, IIT, XLRI, or any partner institute** directly
  - *Example:* Introducing as "I am calling from the IIM Lucknow team" or "I represent the XLRI team"
  - *Example:* Introducing as "Program Manager for IIT Guwahati Executive Program" when the rep is an Accredian employee
- Claiming the **Program Manager or Team Leader is from the partner institute**
  - *Example:* Stating that the TL or screening manager is an IIM/IIT faculty or official
- Impersonating XLRI, IIM, or IIT personnel at any point in the call

#### 2. Compound Multi-Violation Calls
A single call flagged with **multiple serious discrepancies across different violation types** constitutes an L3 incident. Examples of compound violations found in audited calls:
- False identity claim + false career assistance commitment + incorrect batch information — all in one call
- Wrong cohort expectation + placement guarantee + false certification claim — all in one call
- Falsifying identity + incorrect EMI options + false commitment on refund — all in one call

#### 3. Repeated Pattern of Violations
- A rep with **2 or more prior formal warnings** (L1 or L2) who commits any further violation
- A rep where the same category of violation (e.g., introduction failure, refund commitment) recurs **across multiple different leads/calls**

#### 4. Partner Escalation Incidents
- Any case where a **learner or prospect escalates a complaint directly to the partner institute** (IIM, IIT, XLRI, etc.) as a result of the rep's conduct
- Incidents involving **rude, abusive, or threatening behaviour** toward a customer
  - *Example:* Using abusive language over chat or call
  - *Example:* Over-pressuring a customer to register after they have declined

#### 5. Severe Brand Misrepresentation
- Claiming the company name is the **partner institute itself** rather than Accredian
  - *Example:* Introducing as "calling from IIM Visakhapatnam Team Accredian" in a way that implies IIM employment
- Any misrepresentation that could be construed as **fraudulent enrollment practices**

### Actions — Sales Representative
- **Immediate Termination** from the organization
- **Blacklisting** — the individual is barred from future employment at or through Accredian
- **Legal action** initiated where applicable (e.g., identity fraud, contractual breach)
- **Formal internal and external communication** issued to prevent further brand damage

### Actions — Team Lead (TL)
- **Final Review & Leadership Escalation** — TL's performance is placed under senior leadership scrutiny
- **Suspension of 2 days for every L3 case** that occurs under their watch
- **Incentive for the respective month placed on hold** for 1 or more months
- TL must **submit a formal Corrective Action Plan (CAP)** to prevent future violations
- If **multiple L3 offenses** occur under the same TL: termination of the TL

### Actions — ASM / SM and Above (for 2 or more L3 cases in a single month)
- **2-day suspension**
- **Incentive placed on hold** for 1 or more months

### Instance Counter Trigger
- Every L3 incident is recorded as a **critical instance** and triggers immediate escalation.
- No accumulation threshold — a single confirmed L3 incident initiates termination proceedings.

## Escalation Pathway

```
L1 (1st instance)
    → Warning Email + Coaching

L1 (3+ instances, same rep)
    → Flag for L2 review consideration

L2 (1st instance)
    → Suspension (7–14 days) + Formal Review

L2 (4+ instance, same rep)
    → Automatic L3 escalation review

L3 (3+ instances)
    → Immediate Termination + Legal Review (if required)
```

## Flagging Guide for QA Auditors

When reviewing a call recording, apply the following decision sequence:

**Step 1 — Check for L3 triggers first:**
- Is the rep impersonating an IIM/IIT/XLRI representative?
- Is the rep claiming the Program Manager is from the partner institute?
- Are there 4 or more distinct serious violations in the same call?
- Has this rep received 2 or more prior warnings?
- Did a customer escalate directly to a partner institute?

If **YES** to any → Flag as **L3**. Do not downgrade.

**Step 2 — Check for L2 triggers:**
- Is there a false commitment on fees, refunds, or campus immersion costs?
- Is there a false placement/salary hike guarantee?
- Is faculty delivery misrepresented?
- Is the rep claiming wrong alumni status or wrong certification issuer?
- Is eligibility misrepresented or an ineligible candidate being confirmed?
- Is there any exceptional written/verbal commitment outside approved factsheets?

If **YES** to any → Flag as **L2**.

**Step 3 — Check for L1 triggers:**
- Was the company name "Accredian" omitted from the introduction?
- Was the full institute name not used (e.g., "IIT-G" instead of "E&ICT Academy IIT Guwahati")?
- Were program components mentioned vaguely or with incorrect numbers (modules, days, projects)?
- Was attendance policy not mentioned when asked?
- Was script adherence missing or incomplete?

If **YES** to any → Flag as **L1**.

**Step 4 — Compound Violation Rule:**
If a single call has **both L1 and L2 violations** (L1 more than 3 & L2 more than 3 violations) simultaneously (i.e., multiple distinct discrepancy types), elevate the flag to **L3** due to the compound nature of the misconduct.

## Key Definitions

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Mis-selling** | Any act of providing false, incomplete, misleading, or unverified information to a prospective learner with the effect of influencing their enrollment decision |
| **Script Adherence** | Following the standard approved introduction and program description template in every call |
| **False Commitment** | Any promise or assurance made to a prospect that is not supported by the official factsheet, brochure, or policy document |
| **Impersonation** | Representing oneself or another person as an employee, faculty, or official of a partner institute (IIM, IIT, XLRI, etc.) when no such affiliation exists |
| **Incomplete Introduction** | An introduction that omits the company name "Accredian" or the full and correct name of the partner institute |
| **Instance Counter** | A running tally of audited violations per Sales Rep and per Team Lead, used to determine escalation thresholds |
| **Compound Violation** | A single call or case in which two or more distinct violation categories are identified, automatically escalating the flag severity |
| **QA Audit** | A structured review of a sales call recording against defined criteria by a Quality Assurance team member |

## Sales Intelligence & Lost Opportunity Audit
*Auditors must extract these data points to assist in sales coaching and revenue recovery.*

### 1. Customer Persona Extraction
- **Professional Context**: Years of experience, current role, and team size (e.g., "TPM managing 45 people").
- **Financial Baseline**: Current CTC vs. Target/Expected CTC (e.g., "36L → 1Cr").
- **Core Motivation**: Why are they here? (e.g., "Startup founder needing business roadmapping").

### 2. Sales Psychology Assessment
- **Consultative vs. Product-Push**: Did the rep ask "Why" to understand the need, or did they jump straight to the pitch?
- **Hype Authenticity**: Was urgency created through the value of the cohort, or through desperate scholarship threats?
- **Tone/Energy**: Did the rep sound like an expert advisor or a desperate seller?

### 3. Lost Opportunity Analysis
- **Objection Handling**: How did the rep respond to "I am outside" or "I need to think"? Did they set a firm next step or concede control?
- **The "Drop" Reason**: Identify if the call ended because of price, time, authority, or poor rapport.
